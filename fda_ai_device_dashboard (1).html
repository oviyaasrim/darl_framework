AI Device Gap in MAUDE

MAUDE was not designed to capture AI-specific failures. Concept drift, dataset shift, and algorithmic bias errors are largely invisible in current MDR reporting. This is a critical gap your V&V process must compensate for.

AI-Specific Failure Modes NOT Captured by MAUDE
CRITICAL GAP FOR R&D ENGINEERS
Concept Drift
The real-world patient population deviates from the AI training distribution over time, silently degrading performance without triggering a reportable event.
Algorithmic Bias
Subgroup performance disparities (by race, sex, age) are not captured in individual device reports and require population-level surveillance to detect.
Dataset Shift
Changes in imaging equipment, clinical protocols, or EHR workflows can shift input distributions, causing silent AI performance degradation without any device malfunction.

Regulatory Framework Evolution Timeline
FDA AI Policy Milestones
2017
FDA Digital Health Center of Excellence established. SaMD Clinical Evaluation guidance published.
2021
10 Good Machine Learning Practice (GMLP) Guiding Principles released jointly by FDA, Health Canada, UK MHRA.
2024
Final PCCP guidance published Dec 2024. EU AI Act enters force Aug 2024 (high-risk classification for medical AI).
Jan 25
FDA Draft Guidance: TPLC Lifecycle Management for AI-enabled devices. Covers model lineage, bias analysis, human-AI workflow, PCCP.
2026
QMSR replaces QSR, aligning US with ISO 13485. EU AI Act high-risk obligations enforcement begins Aug-2026/27.
2026+
IMDRF GMLP final document (Jan 2025) sets international framework. ISO 22863 AI safety standards for devices in development.
